5. Ada sebuah mic condenser sensitivity = 25mV/Pa (-36dB+-2dB) Jika ada mic yang Sensitivity nya = 17.2mV/Pa berarti berapa dB? ​


Untuk mengkonversi sensitivitas mikrofon dari mV/Pa menjadi dB, kita dapat menggunakan rumus berikut:

dB = 20 * log10(sensitivity)

Untuk mikrofon dengan sensitivitas 17.2mV/Pa, kita akan menghitung nilai dB-nya:

dB = 20 * log10(17.2)

Setelah menghitungnya, kita dapat menggunakan kalkulator atau perangkat lunak untuk mendapatkan hasil yang akurat. Dalam kasus ini, hasilnya sekitar -32.5 dB.

Jadi, mikrofon dengan sensitivitas 17.2mV/Pa memiliki sensitivitas sekitar -32.5 dB.

11. Water is the working fluid in a Carnot vapor power cycle. Saturated liquid enters the boiler at a pressure of 8 MPa, and saturated vapor enters the turbine. The condenser pressure is 8 kPa. Determine (a) the thermal efficiency. (b) the back work ratio. (c) the heat transfer to the working fluid per unit mass passing through the boiler,in kJ/kg. (d) the heat transfer from the working fluid per unit mass passing through the condenser, in kJ/kg


Jawaban:

Penjelasan: To solve this problem, we will use the Carnot vapor power cycle equations and properties of water from steam tables. The Carnot cycle consists of four processes: isentropic compression (1-2), constant pressure heat addition (2-3), isentropic expansion (3-4), and constant pressure heat rejection (4-1).

Given:

- Boiler pressure (p2) = 8 MPa = 8,000 kPa

- Condenser pressure (p4) = 8 kPa

We need to find:

(a) Thermal efficiency (η)

(b) Back work ratio (BWR)

(c) Heat transfer to the working fluid in the boiler (Qin) in kJ/kg

(d) Heat transfer from the working fluid in the condenser (Qout) in kJ/kg

Step-by-step solution:

(a) Thermal efficiency (η):

The thermal efficiency of the Carnot cycle is given by:

η = 1 - (T4 / T2)

First, we need to find the temperatures at states 2 and 4:

- State 2: Saturated liquid at 8 MPa → Find the temperature at this state (T2).

- State 4: Saturated vapor at 8 kPa → Find the temperature at this state (T4).

We can use steam tables or software to find the corresponding temperatures:

- At 8 MPa, the temperature (T2) is approximately 315.19°C.

- At 8 kPa, the temperature (T4) is approximately 45.81°C.

Now, we can calculate the thermal efficiency:

η = 1 - (45.81 + 273.15) / (315.19 + 273.15)

η ≈ 1 - 318.96 / 588.34

η ≈ 1 - 0.542

η ≈ 0.458 or 45.8%

(b) Back work ratio (BWR):

The back work ratio is given by:

BWR = (T2 - T1) / (T3 - T2)
